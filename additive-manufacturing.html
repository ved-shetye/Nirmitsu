What is Additive Manufacturing?

Additive Manufacturing (AM), is a process of creating objects layer by layer from digital designs. Unlike traditional subtractive manufacturing, it adds material only where needed, reducing waste and enabling complex designs.

Additive Manufacturing Technology

Building objects layer by layer from digital models with precise, waste-efficient production using various materials

Fused Deposition Modeling (FDM)

FDM technology creates parts by extruding thermoplastic filaments layer by layer to build up the desired object.

Best suited for:

  • Functional prototypes
  • Manufacturing tools
  • End-use parts
  • Large components

Selective Laser Sintering (SLS)

SLS uses a high-power laser to fuse small particles of polymer powder into a solid structure.

Perfect for:

  • Complex geometries
  • Functional prototypes
  • End-use parts
  • High-strength components

Stereolithography (SLA)

SLA technology uses a laser to cure liquid resin into hardened plastic, creating highly detailed parts.

Ideal for:

  • High detail models
  • Smooth surface finish
  • Visual prototypes
  • Small, intricate parts

Digital Light Processing (DLP)

DLP uses a digital light projector screen to flash a single image of each layer across the entire platform at once.

Excellent for:

  • Fast production
  • High accuracy
  • Dental applications
  • Jewelry casting
